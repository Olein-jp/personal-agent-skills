## Observed Platform Differences

### Olein Design Blog

- More instructional and searchable.
- Titles often include exact WordPress function names, plugin names, or practical outcomes.
- Common structure: intro, table of contents, headings, parameters/features, usage, caveats, summary.
- External references are used to support facts, especially official documentation or plugin pages.
- The writing is calm and teacher-like, but still uses personal judgment in opinion pieces.

### Note

- More personal and reflective.
- Starts from a life event, family context, learning update, or current uncertainty.
- Uses `僕` more openly.
- Allows casual phrasing, parenthetical comments, and small self-corrections.
- Ends with a gentle reflection rather than a hard CTA.

## Reusable Style Signals

- The author often narrows broad claims with conditions: `場合もあります`, `全ての場合ではありません`, `現時点では`.
- The author values operation and maintenance, not only implementation.
- The author explains why a choice matters before explaining how to do it.
- The author is comfortable admitting uncertainty or personal bias.
- The author prefers useful humility over authority performance.
- Technical writing includes exact names and concrete examples.
- Personal writing includes daily constraints, family/work realities, and learning posture.

## Drafting Calibration

When the user asks for:

- A WordPress how-to: use the Olein Design Blog technical article mode.
- A web production opinion piece: combine practical opinion mode with technical article mode.
- A note article: use personal essay mode.
- A service/news post: use announcement mode, but keep it restrained.
- A rough idea: first propose a title and section outline in this style, then draft if requested.
